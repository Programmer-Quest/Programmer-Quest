You fish for so long that you start to lose track of time. The bobber quietly rises and falls with the gentle waves. Your concentration is only shaken by the occasional sound of some fish disturbing the water surface. But you are thrown out of your trance-like state when you notice that every time you catch a fish a red warning message pops up in the chat, stating:

Warning: Inventory full

Oh no! You caught too many fish, and now you do not have enough inventory space to carry them all. Lucky for you, the inventory can be upgraded to increase the amount of items it can carry.

The inventory upgrades follow the Fibonacci sequence, i.e. the next upgrade would increase the inventory space by as much as the previous two upgrades together. So the first and second upgrade would increase the inventory size by 1, the third would increase the size by 2, the fourth by 3, and so on. If the inventory has space for 20 items to start with, you would need to acquire 7 upgrades to be able to hold 50 items: 

  
    20 + 1 + 1 + 2 + 3 + 5 + 8 + 13 = 53

Just as you figure out what the loading screen tip says, the progress bar hits 100% and you are thrown into the game!

The starting zone seems to be typical grassland, with a few trees here and there and a few scattered houses. You can see a few other players running around, probably trying to complete some quest. A few meters in front of you is a conveniently placed NPC with a big exclamation mark above his head, which you correctly guess means that he has a quest for you. Your first quest!

You talk to the NPC, who turns out to be a farmer, aptly named Willem Farmer. He tells you that he has a problem with the rapidly increasing wild boar population destroying his crop. He then asks you kindly to travel to the nearby forest and hunt boars, for which you will be rewarded a few coins for your trouble.

You make your way to the forest, and start hunting the boars. They are spawning pretty slowly, but after a while you realize a pattern: It seems like the boars have a fixed set of spawn points, and every such point has a fixed interval between spawning a new boar. For example, given the following spawn point (format 'x,y: interval'): 


5,3: 5      (sp1)
-2,0: 3     (sp2)
4,4: 7      (sp3)

The fastest way to slay five boars would be sp2 -> sp1 -> sp2 -> sp3 -> sp2, and would mean that you have to travel a total of 26,14 coordinates in Manhattan distance, assuming you start at the spawn point with the lowest interval.

So, given the following points:

  • You start at the spawn point with the lowest interval
  • You start at time '0'
  • It costs no time to move between spawn points
  • It costs no time to slay a boar

How far would you have to travel to slay 1000 boars as fast as possible using the spawn points in the input file? (Enter your answer in the format 'x,y' without quotation marks).
